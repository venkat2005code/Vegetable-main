The Wild Process

The Science of Fermentation

How wild micro-organisms transform simple sugars into rich, complex lactic acid flavor profiles step-by-step.

1

Somatic Preparation & Salting

Fresh vegetables are chopped and massaged with sea salt. The salt draws out moisture, creating a nutrient-rich brine while softening cell walls.

2

Anaerobic Submergence

Vegetables are compressed beneath the brine surface. Eliminating oxygen blocks harmful aerobic molds, selecting only anaerobic bacteria.

3

Leuconostoc Proliferation

During the first 5-7 days, mild *Leuconostoc mesenteroides* bacteria initiate fermentation, producing carbon dioxide and lactic acids.

4

Lactobacillus Dominance

As acidity increases, highly resilient *Lactobacillus plantarum* takes over. This phase produces intense, mature sour flavor compounds.
