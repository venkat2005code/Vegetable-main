The Wild Process

The Science of Fermentation

How wild micro-organisms transform simple sugars into rich, complex lactic acid flavor profiles step-by-step.

1

Somatic Preparation & Salting

Fresh vegetables are chopped and massaged with sea salt. The salt draws out moisture, creating a nutrient-rich brine while softening cell walls.

2

Anaerobic Submergence

Vegetables are compressed beneath the brine surface. Eliminating oxygen blocks harmful aerobic molds, selecting only anaerobic bacteria.

3

Leuconostoc Proliferation

During the first 5-7 days, mild *Leuconostoc mesenteroides* bacteria initiate fermentation, producing carbon dioxide and lactic acids.

4

Lactobacillus Dominance

As acidity increases, highly resilient *Lactobacillus plantarum* takes over. This phase produces intense, mature sour flavor compounds.

Our Heritage

Small Kitchen. Grand Microbes.

Founded in 2021 in a small home pantry, Brine & Barrel Co. was born from a passion for age-old wild fermentation traditions. What started as simple mason jars of cucumbers on kitchen counters has grown into a community fermentary dedicated to clean, raw foods.

We never pasteurize our products, never use artificial preservatives, and source only organic produce. We let the natural bacteria do the work, preserving nutrients and producing superior complex tanginess.

The Standard

The 5 Core Steps to Perfect Brine

How we maintain product excellence across every batch we submerge.

01

Farm Sourcing Inspection

Vegetables are picked and brought to the kitchen within 24 hours. We test texture snap and sugar concentration to ensure organic quality.

02

Manual Slicing & Salting

Produce is hand-prepped, keeping fibers intact. Clean sea salt is massaged in to trigger natural moisture extraction.

03

Submergence & Air-locking

Crated into glass and wood fermenters, fully covered in brine. Custom gas airlocks allow CO2 escape while blocking oxygen.

04

Acidity Curve Monitoring

We trace temperature and pH daily. Only when acidity levels drop below 4.0 pH do we authorize product bottling.

05

Cold Storage Bottling

Packaged raw in amber-tinted jars to block light, then stored at 38°F to decelerate fermentation activity, keeping cultures alive.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about raw ferments, storage, yeast formations, and delivery lines.

Do I need to keep my jars refrigerated?

Yes, absolutely. Because our ferments are raw and unpasteurized, they contain active live cultures. Keeping them cold (around 34-39°F) decelerates fermentation activity, maintaining flavor consistency and carbonation.

What is the white film on top of my jar?

This is typically Kahm yeast—a harmless, wild surface yeast that occasionally develops in low-acid anaerobic environments. You can easily scrape it off; the pickles beneath remain perfectly safe and delicious.

How long do raw ferments keep?

If kept properly refrigerated beneath the brine level, raw ferments can keep easily for 9-12 months. Over time, the flavor profile will become increasingly sour and complex.
